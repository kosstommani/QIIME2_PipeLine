Principal Coordinate Analysis (PCoA) is a technique that helps to extract and visualize a few highly informative components of variation from complex, multidimensional data.
This is a transformation that maps the samples present in the distance matrix to a new set of orthogonal axes such that a maximum amount of variation is explained by the first principal coordinate, the second largest amount of variation is explained by the second principal coordinate.
The two dimensional principal coordinates provide an intuitive visualization of the data structure and look at differences between the samples.
